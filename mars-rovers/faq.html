Frequently Asked Questions

Why does NASA send rovers to Mars?

NASA sends rovers to Mars to study the planet’s surface, climate, geology, and history. These missions help scientists understand whether Mars may have once supported life.

How do Mars rovers communicate with Earth?

Rovers usually send information to orbiters around Mars, which then relay that data back to Earth through NASA’s communication networks.

Why do rovers move so slowly?

Mars rovers move slowly because every path must be carefully planned to avoid rocks, slopes, sand traps, and other hazards that could damage the rover.

Which Mars rovers are still active?

Curiosity and Perseverance are active NASA rovers currently exploring Mars.

What is Perseverance collecting?

Perseverance is collecting rock and soil samples from Jezero Crater. These samples may eventually be returned to Earth for detailed study.

Did Mars ever have water?

Evidence from rover missions shows that Mars once had liquid water, including ancient rivers, lakes, and minerals that formed in wet environments.
