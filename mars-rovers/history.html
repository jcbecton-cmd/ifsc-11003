Mission History

NASA’s Mars rover missions have explored the Red Planet across several decades, helping scientists study ancient water, Martian geology, climate, and the possibility that Mars once had conditions suitable for life.

Spirit and Opportunity

Launched: 2003 | Landed: 2004

Spirit and Opportunity were twin rovers sent to search for evidence of past water activity on Mars. Opportunity became especially famous for lasting nearly 15 years instead of its planned 90-day mission.

Curiosity

Launched: 2011 | Landed: 2012

Curiosity landed in Gale Crater to study whether Mars ever had the right environmental conditions to support microbial life. It continues to analyze rocks, soil, climate, and radiation.

Perseverance

Launched: 2020 | Landed: 2021

Perseverance explores Jezero Crater, searches for signs of ancient life, and collects rock samples that may be returned to Earth by a future mission.
